Network Sniffing – Secure Communication in Networks

How can you protect yourself against network sniffing? Most of you have probably read or heard somewhere that everyone is talking about secure communication. But what is secure communication and why can normal communication channels be intercepted (sniffed)? In this article, I would like to address the topic of “sniffing” and show you how you can protect yourself against such sniffing attacks. Following Edward Snowden’s revelations, you are probably all familiar with the concept of communication channels being tapped. Tapping or sniffing can be done on both a small and large scale. Secret services use entire nodes of our ISPs (Internet Service Providers) for tapping, and on a smaller scale, this can of course also be done in your company or at home. There are, of course, several measures that can be taken to compromise a communication channel. In this article, however, I would like to focus on what is known as “sniffing.”
